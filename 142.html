2.Why does the heavier elements do not form pπ – pπ multiple bond as carbon do?
Ans.
Carbon has the unique ability to form pπ – pπ multiple bond with itself and with other
atoms of small size and high electro negativity whereas heavier elements do not from pπ –
pπ bonds because their atomic orbital’s are too large and diffuse to have effective
overlapping.
3.Why is CO considered poisonous ?
Ans.
The highly poisonous nature of CO arises because of its ability to form a complex with
haemoglobin which is about 300 times more stable than the oxygen – haemoglobin complex.
This prevents haemoglobin in the red blood corpuscles from carrying oxygen round the body
and ultimately resulting in death.
