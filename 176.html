19.(i) In the reaction equilibrium
A+B
C +D,
What will happen to the concentrations of A, B and D if concentration of C is increased.
(ii) what will happen if concentration of A is increased?
Ans. (i)
For an equilibrium reaction
A+B
C+D
If the concentration of a product is increased, the concentration of other components
changes in such a way that the conc of C decreases and vice – versa.
If the conc of C is increased the conc of D will decrease and those of A and B will increase
simultaneously so that the numerical value of Kc is the same and vice – versa. The
equilibrium shifts to the left.
(ii)
If the conc of A is increase, conc of B will decrease and those of C and D will increase
simultaneously so that the numerical value of Kc is the same and vice – versa. The
equilibrium shifts to the right
