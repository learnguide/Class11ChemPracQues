13.Why is ice less dense then water and what kind of attractive forces must be
overcome to melt ice?
Ans.
The structure of ice is an open structure having a number of vacant spaces. Therefore,
the density of ice is less than water. When ice melts the hydrogen bonds are broken and the
water molecules go in between the vacant spaces. As a result, the structure of liquid water is
less open than structure of ice. Thus ice is less dense than
water
