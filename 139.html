9.Why is carbon different from other member of the group?
Ans.
Carbon differs from rest of the members of its group due to its smaller size, higher
electro negativity, higher ionization enthalpy and unavailability of d-orbital’s.
10.Why does the covalence of carbon not expand beyond four?
Ans.
In carbon, only s and p orbital’s are available for bonding and therefore it can
accommodate only four pairs of electrons around it. This limit the maximum covalence to
four whereas other members can expand their covalence due to the presence of d-orbital’s.
